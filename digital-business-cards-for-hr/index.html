Ultimate Success Guide for Digital Business Cards for HR Teams

by Qr Code Editor1 month agono comment

Human Resources (HR) is one of the most crucial departments in all organizations. It’s responsible for making an organization function smoothly. 

Therefore, upgrading HR technology is a priority for all organizations. 60% of the organizations consider increasing HR productivity as their biggest challenge. 

Creating and issuing employee business cards is one of the major HR responsibilities.

Business cards are essential for all employees, especially sales and marketing reps. However, standard business cards have drawbacks. They have limited space, and employees can’t share all the information.

The contact-saving process is also tedious. Users have to manually enter the name and phone number, which is time-consuming and error-prone.

Poor shelf-life and no editing capabilities are some other disadvantages of business cards.

Here’s where digital business cards come into the picture. Digital business cards provide substantial advantages over physical business cards. And by integrating the two, you can take your employees’ networking abilities to the next level.

What are digital business cards?

A digital business card is a web page containing an employee’s information. This  includes their personal and professional information, contact details, images, and social media profiles. It’s a digital medium for sharing contact information across channels and building more connections. 

Every digital business card has a unique URL. Employees can share their business card URLs via email, social media, etc., to bring people to their digital business cards. 

That said, printing a QR code on a business card is the best way to bring people to a digital business card. HR departments can issue employee business cards with QR codes printed on them.

Each QR code is unique and redirects to the digital business card of the card owner. This way, you can integrate physical and digital business cards make networking easier for employees.

How does a digital business card work?

A digital business card helps employees share their contact. Users can access a digital business card on any smartphone, tablet, laptop, or desktop. 

Here’s how a digital business card works:

  1. You create digital business cards for your employees and print the QR codes on their business cards. 
  2. Employees share their business cards with potential clients and consumers.
  3. Consumers scan the QR code to reach an employee’s digital business card.
  4. They get information of the employee and contact them.
  5. Employees seamlessly share their contact details and grow their network. 

Key components of a digital business card

Though every digital business card is different, here are some details every digital business card should have:

  • Full name and occupation/profession details
  • A profile summary
  • Contact details, including email, phone number, Fax, etc., and the full address
  • The employee’s profile image and other necessary multimedia
  • Links to social media profiles, especially LinkedIn and Twitter
  • Web links, such as company URLs
  • Other important text

QRCodeChimp provides complete control over the components of your digital business cards. You can add, delete, and move the components as per your needs. 

How can HR teams use digital business cards: Use cases and applications

Digital business cards have various applications in the corporate world. If you want to digitize employee ID cards, digital business cards have got you covered. 

Here’s how HR departments can use digital business cards to enhance HR efficiency and employee productivity. 

ID cards for new employees

HR teams need to print and assign ID cards to new employees. The average cost of printing an employee ID card is $20, and it could vary depending on the order size, design requirements, etc. 

Moreover, physical cards have drawbacks. There’s  limited space and no real-time editing. Hence, organizations could struggle to generate the desired ROI from physical cards. 

HR teams can resolve this challenge by using digital business cards as employee ID cards. Instead of issuing clunky ID cards with a lot of text, you can offer modern, simplistic ID cards with QR codes printed on them.

Upon scanning the QR codes, users will get redirected to employees’ digital business cards. 

Networking tool for employees

Networking is essential for all employees, especially those who directly meet and connect with customers. They need to frequently share their contact details with various consumers. 

Business cards are the go-to way of sharing contact information, but they make the contact sharing and saving process tedious. Users need to manually enter an employee’s contact details, which is time-consuming and can lead to errors. This can even result in a lost networking opportunity. 

Digital business cards make the contact-saving process fast and hassle-free. Hence, employees can build more connections and grow their networks. 

Effective tool for marketing and sales teams

Sales and marketing teams need to connect with consumers to generate leads and conversions. The more leads they generate, the more sales they drive.

However, traditional business cards could slow down the networking process. They can also hinder the ability of sales and marketing teams to generate qualified leads. 

In fact, 88% of business cards handed out are tossed away within a week. That’s a massive loss of networking opportunities. 

Digital business cards are more actionable, and they can help sales and marketing reps generate more leads. They speed up the contact-saving process and help sales and marketing teams connect with more prospects. 

Store and maintain employee information

Maintaining employee data can be challenging, especially in large organizations. In fact, HR teams spend over 30% of their time on administrative tasks. 

HR departments can use digital business cards to save employee information, like their educational qualifications and previous experiences. 

As a result, you don’t have to save too much data in your employee database system. You can have a simple database containing an employee’s name or code and their digital business card URL.

To access an employee’s information, you just need to click on their digital business card URL. 

This way, HR teams can also save technology costs. When you create digital business cards, the data is stored on the vendor’s server. Hence, you don’t have to worry about storage costs, security, and uptime. 

Benefits of digital business cards for HR teams

Digital business cards can create tangible value for the HR department and the entire organization. Here’s why HR teams should incorporate digital business cards in their strategy. 

Improve professionalism and corporate identity

HR teams must maintain and promote a solid and consistent corporate identity. It plays a vital role in attracting new employees and investors.

Furthermore, it goes a long way in attracting customers and building loyalty. Digital business cards enable employees to connect with clients in a professional manner.

They also enhance your corporate identity and differentiate your organization from its competitors. 

Enable seamless contact sharing

The most significant benefit of a digital business card is quick and hassle-free contact sharing. The drill remains the same for employees — they give their business cards to potential consumers. However, how the consumers engage with the cards changes dramatically. 

Instead of entering the employee’s name and phone number, they just need to scan the business card QR code. Upon scanning, they’ll reach the digital business card, where they can obtain the employee’s information and save their contact.

This speeds up the contact-saving process and minimizes the risk of errors. 

Generate more leads and sales

Digital business cards enable sales and marketing teams to build more connections and generate more leads. With digital business cards, reps can share their contact details in a flash and connect with more qualified prospects.

Edit employee information without reprinting

You can edit the information on digital business cards without changing the page URL or reprinting the QR code or business card. Hence, you can reduce printing costs and the environmental impact associated with it.

Discontinue employee cards after they resign

Employees who resign may walk away without handing over their business cards. They could misuse it later, which could hurt your organization. 

When you create digital business cards for employees and enable white labeling, the power is in your hands. You can disable digital business cards with a single click, eliminating the risk of misuse. 

Note: You can control employee digital business cards only if you use a white label URL for the digital business card pages.

Track views on digital business cards

Most digital business card solutions have an analytics dashboard. Keep an eye on the analytics to understand how your digital business cards are performing.

These analytics can help HR teams track the performance of their employees and make informed decisions.
